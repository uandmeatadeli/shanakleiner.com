Shana Kleiner

Shana Kleiner is a researcher and policy analyst studying digital expressions of grief, online communities, and cyber-embodiment. With a strong foundation in social work values, she received her Masters in Social Work from Columbia University, with a focus in Advanced Generalist Practice & Programming. Shana was a policy analyst at a nonprofit that provided free legal services for survivors of domestic and sexual violence, where she co-authored a policy brief on barriers to survivors seeking safety with law enforcement. She is currently the Assistant Director of SAFELab, a transdisciplinary research group drawing on qualitative and computational methods to examine well-being, healthy equity, and social justice with youth of color and marginalized communities. Shana's main research at SAFELab consists of online grief, social work in data science, and bias within large language models. She supervises graduate students, organizes lab activities, manages a number of projects, and conducts creative outreach. Shana was a previous fellow at the MDOCS Storytellers' Institute, and worked with other artists, writers, and filmmakers surrounding the notion of co-creation. Her personal interests include vintage shopping, collaging, and swimming.
